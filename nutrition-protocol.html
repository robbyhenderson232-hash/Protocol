3.3 Eliminated Permanently
  • Sugar in cappuccinos — glucose spike before food destabilises blood sugar and raises cortisol.
  • Ketchup — Heinz is 23% sugar.
  • Hellmann's Light mayo — ~200–300 kcal/day of low-quality oil, no nutritional benefit. Replaced with olive oil drizzle and/or pesto.
  • Diet Coke — phosphoric acid interferes with calcium absorption. Replaced with Pellegrino.
  • Liquid IV electrolytes — 11g sugar per serving. No electrolyte product currently used; plain water on waking.
3.4 — Mayo calorie replacement, added to every day
  • Extra half avocado at breakfast — whole avocado on training days. ~120 kcal, quality monounsaturated fat.
  • 30g almonds and cashews with the afternoon snack on training days — ~180 kcal, 6g protein. Lives in the desk drawer, home and office.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil drizzled over lunch and dinner where mayo would have gone — ~120 kcal/tbsp.
3.5 Supplement Stack
Morning — with breakfast, every day
  • Vitamin D3 5,000IU + K2 100mcg — Thorne or Nature's Best. Take with fat-containing food for absorption.
  • Omega-3 — Bare Biology Lion Heart, 2 capsules. 1,000mg+ EPA/DHA combined. Avoid cheap supermarket omega-3 — EPA/DHA content too low to be therapeutic.
  • Creatine Monohydrate 5g — reduced from 8g; evidence shows identical muscle saturation at 5g.
Evening — 60–90 min before bed
  • Magnesium Glycinate 400mg — Solgar or Nature's Best specifically, not magnesium oxide. Lives next to the toothbrush.
  • Zinc Picolinate 15mg — Solgar. 30 minutes after magnesium; they compete for absorption if taken together.
Pre-training
  • Beet It Sport Nitrate 400 — one shot, 60–90 min before training. Among the strongest evidenced sports nutrition products for nitric oxide and performance.
3.6 Water Protocol
  • On waking — 500ml plain water, before caffeine or food.
  • During training — 500–750ml, sipped every 10–15 min. Don't wait until thirsty.
  • With every meal — 500ml, sipped throughout.
  • With every snack — 300–500ml.
  • Before bed — 200ml small glass only, to avoid a sleep-disrupting bathroom trip.
  • System — two 1-litre bottles, MORNING and AFTERNOON, both finished by 6pm.
3.7 Functional Foods Reference

Mushrooms, tomatoes, avocado

  • Mushrooms — full punnet in the omelette every morning. Vitamin D, potassium, B vitamins.
  • Tomatoes — already in the omelette. Lycopene, a powerful antioxidant.
  • Avocado — whole on training days, half on rest days. Monounsaturated fat, potassium, magnesium.

Rocket, kale, spinach, broccoli

  • Rocket — large handful at lunch. Highest dietary nitrate of any common food — directly increases nitric oxide production for training pump and blood flow.
  • Kale — already at lunch, increase volume. Indole-3-carbinol supports a healthy oestrogen ratio.
  • Broccoli — already in evening meals; request frequently. Same indole-3-carbinol benefit.
  • Spinach — substitute wherever rocket isn't available. Similar nitrate and magnesium profile.

Berries & beetroot shots

  • Blueberries/strawberries — handful with mid-morning snack and post-workout shake. Reduce exercise-induced muscle damage, accelerate recovery.
  • Beet It Sport shots — 400mg dietary nitrate, 60–90 min pre-training. Tesco, Amazon UK, Holland & Barrett.
